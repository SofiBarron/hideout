#WhatNext?: A Case for Persona 5

Are you ready to start the game?

by Amy Aker | Published on May 4th

E xpect the unexpected - or so the saying goes and Shin Megami Tensei: Persona 5 is definitely "unexpected". As one of the flag titles from Atlus, this game may as well be one of their greatest hits for good reason. But, do let me convince you as to why this game is such a good pick.

Following its family, Shin Megami Tensei: Persona 5 is a JRPG that mixes school life with mysteries that ultimately lead to the characters using "personas" - representation of their souls in a way based on the tarot system. If all of this sounds a bit strange or out of the box, hang with me for a bit, and let me present you with the reasons why you should play it.

Convicted

What crime did we commit? Why are we being interrogated? Is there no way out? If anything, the game packs a punch with just a couple of minutes in, where we are, what have we done, why did we do it. Many are the questions that can be raised as we face the matter of whether to give up our name or not.

Hero or Felon?

Either by mistake or bad sheer bad luck, we somehow get mixed in a series of deaths that may be connected in some way or another with people's deepest selves, their hidden desires. To change the course, to change reality and, in turn, break perceptions, how far would we go?

Sucker Punched

While investigating what is going on, there's the high school aspect of the game. Paying attention to class, acing exams, and not raising much suspicion as to extra-curricular activities are part of the ins and outs. Of course, social life is key too, what we do may aid the course of the investigation.

Personas

Last but not least is the persona factor. To dwell in people's most inner thoughts and live to tell the tale, the personifications of our will come to play as we try to grasp the workings of a mind and the consequences of messing with one. These mind palaces are tricky places, though, and nothing is as appears, for even inside a mind we might find ourselves between a rock and a hard place.

Conclusion

All in all, Shin Megami Tensei: Persona 5 is one of those games that effortlessly pulls you in, facing us with moral questions that appear, at first, ever so vague. Are you ready to start the game?
