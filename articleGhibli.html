Studio Ghibli: What does the future hold?

Will Ghibli turn to CGI animation?

by Marian Blue | Published on April 25th

C GI has been around for a while now. From films like Toy Story, How to Train Your Dragon all the way to Spider-Man: Into the Spiderverse, many a studio has honed its skills to bring about features that challenge the concept of what we know as 3D animation. However, one more studio appears to be trying to put a foot in such a world. You may not believe it, but we’re referring to Studio Ghibli with its latest movie: Earwig and The Witch.

Hard as it may be to believe it, the great house that has brought us titles like Spirited Away, Princess Mononoke and Ponyo has ventured to the world of 3D before, but never before in the form of a full movie. Until now. Indeed, it is not one of Hayao Miyazaki’s films, but one of his son’s projects which have received this new treatment breaking away from the tradition. In the form of Earwig and The Witch, we find animation that resembles in style to other Studio Ghibli’s films and tries to join the ranks of what appears to be the future in animation.

If we look closely, however, we will find that the style may be there, but a bit of the magic has been lost in the translation. Now, this is the first attempt at an animation the studio is not known for, and as such, it has been treated as a proof of concept. That does not mean the movie is entirely bad, just that it may not offer the same subtleties other CGI movies do. Nor does it make viewers feel that emotional link to the characters plotwise. A mixture that may leave fans feeling a bit let down.

Whether Studio Ghibli will have another go to this specific way of creating films, remains unknown. What is certain is that it has made its first plunge into a world filled with possibility for the future. Indeed, Earwig and The Witch may not be exactly what was expected in terms of both animation and plot, it certainly marks a precedent. And, who knows, maybe Earwig is just the start of something new.
