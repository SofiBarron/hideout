#AnimePicks: Cowboy Bebop

See you soon, Space Cowboy.

by Balthazar Smith | Published on April 28th

S Some odd years ago, a channel by the name Locomotion started airing its content on cable TV. It was one of those channels that almost completely to animation, and most of it to anime. It was filled with classics like Neon Genesis Evangelion, Submarine Super 99 or Robotech. There was one, though, that has become an unsung favourite, and that’s Cowboy Bebop.

From the mind of Hajime Yatate comes this incredible space opera that takes place in the year 2077. In a world where space travel is as common as taking the train, the occasional robot and cryogenics have been developed we find ourselves with a crew focused on bounty hunting as a paying job. To make such ambience believable, the show takes on an incredible work on the design for ships, what life on other planets could be like and, of course, how the bounties are communicated.

But the main focus of the anime is not the year 2077, but the crew of the Bebop itself. This group of outcasts made up of Jet, Spike, Faye as well as the incredible duo of Ein and Ed is one of those dysfunctional families, we cannot help but love. And there’s a good reason for it. While each episode will focus on some mission, the core of the plot will go through each character’s background, aspirations, and dreams. Because that’s what Cowboy Bebop is ultimately about: humanity in a world that appears completely different to our own.

All these aspects sum up to create one of the animations that have aged best thanks to astounding work on worldbuilding. One that has been done to make travelling in space, visiting new planets, and ultimately living in the future believable. In truth, though, what makes the show stand tall is its take on humanity. The way the character’s story intertwines with each episode’s plot creating a balanced and nuanced series that cannot be easily forgotten. So much so, that the closing words still echo in our mind: See you soon, Space Cowboy.
