#HeroesSunday: Invincible

A new spin on the usual hero formula.

by Clay Rodriguez | Published on April 28th

H eroes have been on the rise lately. Thanks to the constant stream of content from the Marvel Cinematic Universe as well as the DC Universe, plenty of stories have been translated from comic book to screen. However, these two powerhouses are not the only ones, as this week we shall focus on a less known hero by Robert Kirkman, creator of the Walking Dead. We are referring to the flashy new show Invincible.

Thanks to Amazon Video and Skybound Entertainment, we can return to adult swim for a different kind of story on heroes. This time around, our protagonist is a seventeen-year-old kid, Mark Grayson, who happens to be the son of the most powerful hero out there, Omni-Man. Now, everything appears to be closely related to our usual origin stories: Kid is the son of a super-hero, he will probably get powers and ultimately save the world. And yes, it may ring true as episodes go by.

But Invincible offers a much more complex outlook on the realities of super-hero life. It dives head on to the gory scenes of people dying, the struggles of fitting in, the dangers of facing the unknown and the thirst of wanting more, more of everything. Thanks to its double plotline, the show keeps its watchers hooked to the TV. One after the other, bits and pieces of what is going on with Omni-man as well as Mark’s struggles and hardships start forming a full picture. All leave us just to wonder, what will happen next.

In other words, Invincible breaks the mould by bringing up a more mature take on superhero tales. While the formula may remain the same, some of the characters may remind us of others, the plot builds up perfectly on each episode. For nothing is exactly as it seems in this gory, layered show.
